2021 Bentley Flying Spur W12 Review

2021 Bentley Flying Spur W12 Review

Although no longer in production, the 6.0-liter W12 engine enjoyed a prosperous tenure in Bentley's lineup, being the automaker's cornerstone for two decades. Bentley's decision to discontinue it reflects a shift towards sustainable vehicle development, prioritizing environmental responsibility. Currently, the hybridized V6 and the Audi-sourced V8 remain in the lineup, with potential hybridization in line with the brand's aim to become fully electric by 2030. While hybridization promises efficiency, the W12's unparalleled refinement and robustness are irreplaceable. Despite its origins outside Bentley, it became emblematic of the brand. As one of the final model years featuring this engine, the 2021 Bentley Flying Spur W12 warrants examination of its performance capabilities.

Performance That Almost Defies Physics

What lies under the 2021 Bentley Flying Spur's hood is in the name itself, so let's talk performance figures. 626 horsepower and 664 lb.ft is enough to send the car to 60 mph from a standstill in 3.5 seconds, a second quicker than the Flying Spur V8 S, as tested by Car and Driver. As the numbers climb on the speedometer, you're faced with the challenge of reaching the 207 mph top speed, a task effortlessly accomplished thanks to the smooth and precise of the 8-speed dual-clutch transmission and the confident grip provided by the permanent all-wheel-drive system. Amidst the thrill, you might doubt Bentley's precision in estimating the car's weight at around 5,400 pounds.

Pure Luxury, Refinement, And Comfort

There's no Bentley that is short of luxury and comfort in the cabin, and the 2021 Bentley Flying Spur W12 upholds this tradition in every stitch and surface. The cabin sits five if you get the second row as a bench-style seat or four if you get the captain-style bucket seats added as an option in 2020. Leather flows through every surface, perfectly complemented by meticulously chosen wood veneers that enhance the cabin's opulent ambiance. However, you can also get an example with carbon fiber or metal finishes, as this was added to the interior options in 2021. Attention to detail is evident in every aspect, ensuring each surface is visually striking. The dashboard boasts an array of chrome/real metal switchgear and accents. The integration of the infotainment display is well-executed, avoiding any sense of intrusion through the optional rotating feature that can show a wooden veneer or the screen itself.

Every Curve Whispers Luxury

One of the advantages of buying a third-generation Bentley Flying Spur, such as the 2021 Flying Spur W12, is that it is more distinct from the Continental GT compared to the first and second generations. The most evident change is the grille design, which adopts the vane style seen on the Bentley Mulsanne and is larger, leaving less space on each side where it meets the headlights. Bentley's signature bulking hood design accentuates the front end, and where the hood meets the grille sits the Flying B hood mascot, with a chrome strip running from it to the windscreen. Chrome extends to the door sills, window surrounds, door handles, and exhaust tips, adding a pleasing visual touch to the overall design, and the expansive yet elegantly flowing contours accentuate the car's luxurious status.
