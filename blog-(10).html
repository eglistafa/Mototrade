Ferrari Roma Review

It came with a shocking 612 horsepower thanks to the V8 twin-turbo connected to a rear-mounted dual clutch 8-speed auto transmission. The front seats and cabin feel comfortable and spacious. History: Where it Started

The Ferrari Roma was introduced in 2019 and while the Roma was designed on the same platform as the Ferrari Portofino and carried the same 3.9 Liter V8 as well as gearbox, it would feature a totally different exterior and interior design. Ferrari by now had learned a thing or two about building mid-lying front-engine sports cars and reserved this design for some of their fastest models. While the Roma would be showcased as an entry-level model, it didn’t by any means make it a slow car. The car was extremely fast and featured a 2+2 sitting design. It would also come with plenty of tech resulting in a complete package that would offer customers everything they needed. This body style and designed engineering would remain pretty much the same as the current model even though the 2024 Ferrari Roma does come with a few elements that make it unique. If you are wondering where is the Roma made, this happens at the company factory in Maranello, Italy. Now let’s get straight into our Ferrari Roma review.

What's New For 2024

For 2024 the Roma now offers a convertible version. According to Ferrari, the drop top adds just 185 pounds of weight and can lower in just 13.5 seconds when cruising at speed of up to 37 mph. Interior Design: Very Stylish

The Roma interior comes with plenty of leather upholstery and swede-sprinkled sections as well. You get high-end materials which is what anyone would expect with a car of this price. At the front, the driver and passenger seem to have their dashboard section that curves right into the doors. The instrument cluster on the driver’s side is huge with a 16 inch screen. Performance: Exhilarating Yet Controlled!

The Roma features a long hood and it's built that way for a reason, it houses the twin-turbo V8 3.9 Liter engine with horsepower and torque figures sitting at 612 hp and 561 lb-ft respectively. This motor connects to an eight-speed auto transmission that was first installed on the Ferrari SF90 Stradale. The Ferrari Roma 0-60 mph time is just 3.1 seconds and will reach 100 mph in 7 seconds with a top speed of nearly 200 mph. As soon as you press on the gas pedal, the Ferrari Roma responds instantly which would make you forget for a second that there are two turbos connected to the engine. The Roma handles corners like a pro as well, being instantly responsive and direct while also being sharp and accurate allowing the car to enter into those corners with precision. The car will respond at even the slightest turn-off of the steering wheel and many drivers may require some time to get used to the sheer accuracy of the steering. As for the suspension, it feels harder on the Ferrari Roma than say, on the Bentley Continental GT which means it may not handle the corrugations on the roads as well as the Bentley. That said, Ferrari more than makes up for this when it comes to utter wheel grip and handling. Overall, the Roma weight sits at 3245 lbs.
